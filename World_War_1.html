America in World War 1

Neutrality

The USA initially remained neutral in the war when it first broke out. The USA held sympathetic feelings towards both the central and allied powers, causing the American people to want to remain neutral in the war, especially the 10% ethnic German population.

Unrestricted U-Boat Warfare (Sussex, Aztec sink)

American sympathies towards Germany began to decline once Germany began its policy of unrestricted submarine warfare, where they sunk passenger ships such as the Lusitania in May of 1915 killing nearly 1,200 passengers, including 128 Americans. The sinking of the Lusitania increased tensions between the USA and Germany causing the Germans to end their unrestricted submarine warfare policy...
